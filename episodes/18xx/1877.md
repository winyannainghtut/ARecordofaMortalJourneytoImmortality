# A Record of a Mortal’s Journey to Immortality - Chapter 1877

However, to create a Beetle Monarch, the 13 needed to keep devouring each other, which naturally frustrated Han Li. Since he could not find a good solution, he could only use whatever methods he had to refine their bodies for now.

Doing so would gradually improve their powers, increasing the long-term chance of eventually producing a true Beetle Monarch.

Even though he had failed to produce one yet, he could already control these 13 Purple-striped Gold Devouring Beetles (ခရမ်းရောင် အစင်းပါ ရွှေရောင် စားသုံး ပိုးကောင်) effortlessly with his Spiritual Sense (ဝိညာဉ် အာရုံ). They were now a far more practical combat option than tens of thousands of ordinary Gold Devouring Beetles and became a new trump card in his collection.

In the past years he had also largely exhausted his stock of Divine Infernal River Elixir, and his physical constitution had changed profoundly. The most obvious change was that the speed at which he could convert origin Qi into magic power during cultivation had increased by over 30 percent.

Though 30 percent may not sound huge, it effectively meant that achievements that once required three units of time now took only two.

Besides that, Qing Yuanzi had earlier told him that cleansing the body with the elixir would be extremely helpful for breaking through Body Integration Stage bottlenecks. Otherwise, demon monarchs of the Earth Abyss would not have risked entering the human world to obtain even some of it.

Han Li had also constantly taken pills that enhanced his cultivation base, so his magic power was rising rapidly.

At this point he had already reached the peak of mid-Body Integration Stage and needed less than 20 more years of preparation before attempting breakthrough.

With that in mind he sighed softly and closed his eyes to resume cultivation. But at that exact moment, a flash of white spiritual light burst from the door of the secret chamber, followed by a clear wave of pale light filling it.

Seeing this, Han Li slightly trembled and immediately swept his sleeve to the door. The seal of spiritual light opened instantly.

A fireball immediately shot into the chamber and quickly entered Han Li's grasp. He stared at it briefly, then sent his spiritual sense inside. His expression changed dramatically.

His fingers clacked together and with a dull thud he crushed the fireball. Under its red glow an extremely dark look spread across his face.

"I didn't expect the devilish mark to appear so soon. It seems I have no choice but to leave seclusion now," he muttered as spiritual light erupted from his body.

The other two Han Lis, seeing this, turned expressionless and flew toward him in paired projections. All three merged as one in a low echo and Han Li's aura suddenly swelled dramatically as he rose.

He waved at the giant azure cauldron; it rapidly shrank to human-fist size and vanished into his sleeve in a streak of azure light.

Then Han Li lifted his head and instantly appeared at the entrance of the secret chamber, covering more than a hundred feet in an instant. He strode out and vanished.

Within a mountain range in the primordial world, at the foot of a colossal mountain, a white-robed youth faced three gigantic primordial beasts.

One of the beasts was a two-headed giant over a thousand feet tall. Its terrifying figure was made even more grotesque by disheveled yellow hair on its heads; one hand held a huge black club.

The other two were enormous crimson pythons, one much larger than the other. The larger one exceeded three thousand feet in length, while the smaller one was about 700-800 feet.

Both exhaled toxic red mist from their mouths, and all three looked cautiously at the small youth.

The white-robed youth had strange golden and silver runes on his face and spoke in a cold tone,

"Hehe, I warned everyone in this place to leave a few days ago. All sensible beings already left; only you three chose to refuse. Do you really think you can oppose me by forming a band together? How naïve! In that case, you needn't linger longer. I will take your souls and inner cores and make this your final resting place!"

The three beasts seemed to possess some intelligence. As soon as they heard this, the two-headed giant clenched its teeth, then slammed a giant fist into its own chest, threw back its head, and released a furious snarl.

The two crimson pythons also had cold eyes and flicked their forked tongues faster as their hissing grew louder and harsher.

A chilly smile appeared on the youth's face. He formed a hand seal and vanished downward.

A dull thud sounded as a burst of white glacial Qi spread, covering several acres instantly. In moments, countless glittering translucent egg-sized snowflakes appeared in the air.

From that white Qi came a sharp cry. A pristine white centipede-like ice spirit rushed out.

It had six wings and a pair of bright-red eyes. Initially around 70–80 feet long, it elongated in an instant beyond a thousand feet and opened its mouth to spew white glacial flames, which surged as a white glacial sea toward the three beasts.

The beasts jolted. They immediately unleashed their own defenses, understanding how fearsome this white Qi was.

The two-headed giant gripped its black club with both hands and swung it violently through the air. Explosions rippled in sequence as a fierce wind rushed toward the distant glacial Qi.

At the same time the two pythons opened their cavernous mouths and sent great bursts of red mist that turned into scorching flames, shaping a fiery barrier around their bodies.

But under the white glacial sea, the fierce wind and flames were instantly crushed. The three beasts, in surprise and horror, were frozen into ice statues before they could react.

The massive ice centipede circled above them and in a flurry of wingbeats released six white streaks of light that spiraled around the three statues like bolts. A resounding boom rang out and the statues crumbled into piles of ice flakes.

Light flashed. The three giant beasts' souls rose and immediately tried to flee in panic, but the centipede opened its mouth once more, releasing an invisible suction force that instantly engulfed the area and swept up both their frozen remnants and their souls to be devoured.

Soon white light flashed again, and the white-robed youth reappeared where the centipede had vanished.

He looked down with an icy expression, confirmed nothing remained, then turned his gaze to the towering mountain ahead with a fierce gleam.

His body trembled once, and he shot through the air as a streak of white light, disappearing into the mountain after several flashes.

A short while later, he reappeared inside a deep cave beneath the mountain. He stared at a rugged reddish-purple stone wall with obvious excitement.

"Haha, I have found a Purple Yin Ore mine. Looks like I'll obtain a true spirit body sooner than expected!"

His voice trailing off, he instantly dropped down and shifted back into centipede form. This time the centipede was only a few tens of feet long and emitted a flare of light that enveloped the wall.

The wall slowly vanished in white spiritual radiance, leaving behind a giant black gaping hole.

Immediately the six-winged centipede flapped and vanished into the hole as a streak of black light; rumbling booms were heard from inside.

In a deep-sea palace secret chamber, there hovered a giant cocoon wrapped in endless crimson threads, writhing constantly. Crimson runes appeared across its surface and it began to expand at an incredible speed.

Within a single breath the entire chamber filled with it, then a faint black shadow inside the cocoon shifted.

The cocoon exploded in an earth-shattering boom, and a naked young man appeared in midair.

His skin was like polished jade and his features were exceptionally handsome. Bright golden lotus markings were written on both front and back of his body.

The flowers, only a few feet in size, were vivid and almost living; they spread over nearly all of him and their mere sight induced a dizzy sensation.

His eyes were tightly closed, expression calm. For some time he hovered unmoving.

After a long while, faint golden light flashed from his body and the lotus patterns twisted and stirred as if alive.

The young man slowly opened his eyes, revealing a pair of dazzling pupils with strange power.

Two dull thumps sounded, and two fireballs ignited within his eyes—one golden, the other silver, both flickering as he moved his head.

His gaze landed on an object in a corner, and a peculiar look appeared on his face.

There it was: a crystal ball on a short jade table. The sphere, about the size of a human fist, shimmered with faint spiritual light and reflected a clear image inside.

The image displayed a shimmering silver lotus seedpod, strikingly lifelike and realistic.
